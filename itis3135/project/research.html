Research Experience

Since 2024, I have served as a research volunteer in the Biodynamics Lab at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte. This hands-on experience has deepened my understanding of musculoskeletal and performance physiology while allowing me to work directly with Division I student-athletes and graduate-level researchers.

In this role, I supported a range of biomechanical research activities, with a focus on ultrasound imaging to evaluate muscle and tendon structures. By assisting with the collection of high-quality data, I contributed to ongoing studies aimed at improving athletic performance, injury prevention, and rehabilitation protocols.

Key responsibilities and skills gained include:

  • Assisting with the setup, calibration, and operation of ultrasound imaging equipment
  • Collaborating with graduate students during live data collection sessions
  • Maintaining proper documentation and organization of collected data
  • Developing familiarity with human biomechanics and soft tissue imaging
  • Practicing strict adherence to research ethics and confidentiality standards

This opportunity has not only strengthened my technical and teamwork skills but has also sparked a deeper interest in clinical research and its impact on sports medicine and rehabilitation science.
